Academic career

1990-1996
Master and Doctoral Degrees obtained at University of Grenoble, France, under the supervision of Dominique Borrione. Main research interest: symbolic-model checking techniques and applications to hardware design.
1995-1997
Visiting researcher/post-doc at Carnegie Mellon University, with Prof Ed Clarke. Main research interest: symbolic model checking applied to VHDL.
1997-now
Professor at Federal University of Rio Grande do Norte, Brazil. Main research interests: SMT solving, formal methods.
2002-2003
Sabbatical at LORIA, France, in the research team CASSIS, with Prof. Michael Rusinowitch. Main research interest: SMT solving.
2013-2014
Sabbatical at LORIA, France, in the research team Veridis, with Prof. Stephan Merz. Main research interest: SMT solving.
